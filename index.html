01Software engineering· Industry placement, Loopsio · 2026

ALICS: productionising computer vision for structural inspection

In one line: I built the application layer that turned three research crack-detection models into a desktop product that non-technical surveyors install and run like any normal app.

Role Software developer (team of 4) Duration 16 weeks Evidence demo video below

Problem. Three research computer-vision models (YOLO detection, U-Net segmentation, photometric-stereo 3D reconstruction) existed only as lab code. Civil engineers needed to run calibrated crack analysis offline, on site, and export engineering-grade reports, without installing Python or touching a terminal.

Approach. I built the entire application layer around the pre-existing analysis code: a Tauri 2 / Rust backend, a React 19 + TypeScript frontend, and a zero-copy Rust-to-Python inference bridge using PyO3 with NumPy ndarray views, so multi-megapixel images cross the language boundary without serialisation. A 12-migration SQLite layer persists per-scan, per-cell analysis results, including a BLOB migration that resolved row-size limits on large scans.

Outcome. Shipped as signed installers for Windows, macOS and Linux with a fully bundled Python runtime. The tool outputs calibrated crack-width measurements in millimetres, a Three.js 3D mesh viewer with a signed depth scale, and templated PDF inspection reports suitable for structural documentation.

02Operations research· Coursework, Mathematical Programming in Advanced Analytics · 2026

School connectivity optimisation under budget uncertainty

In one line: a multi-period MILP and two-stage stochastic programme allocating a $125M national budget to connect 12,578 Honduran schools, solved twice to put a price on political deployment milestones.

Role Geospatial pipeline & modelling (team project) Solver Gurobi via Julia/JuMP Evidence interactive dashboard below

Problem. Roughly 93% of Honduran public schools have no internet connection. Connectivity requires a three-layer prerequisite chain (tower, then WISP equipment, then school devices) with an 18:1 cost gap between equipping an existing tower and building a new one, phased over five years against an uncertain budget.

Approach. Our team formulated a multi-period mixed-integer linear programme: budgeted maximal covering, prerequisite sequencing constraints, soft annual deployment milestones, and a two-stage stochastic extension hedging a ±20% budget shock in years 3 to 5. I built the geospatial data pipeline over 15,000+ schools and 7,297 candidate tower sites using a 10 km Haversine coverage radius.

Outcome. We solved the model twice on purpose: once unrestricted, connecting 98.7% of in-scope schools, and once with political deployment milestones, which costs 24.3 percentage points of final coverage, roughly 125 schools per point of early visibility. Both solutions are embedded in the dashboard below; switching scenario shows the 3,046-school gap materialise on the map. The stochastic extension adds a 1,067-school robust core immune to budget cuts, with EVPI and VSS bounds of at least 263 and 177 schools.

03Machine learning & statistics· Personal project · 2026

World Cup 2026 bracket predictor

In one line: a LightGBM + Poisson ensemble built leakage-free on 96 years of history that beats the baseline in every one of nine held-out tournaments.

Role Solo, data pipeline to validation Validation Leave-one-tournament-out CV · 100k Monte Carlo

Problem. Sports prediction is notorious for leakage and overfitting: features quietly built on future information, and test tournaments that bleed into training. The design constraint here was generalisation first, predictive power second.

Approach. Custom regex state machines parse 1,402 matches across every World Cup since 1930 plus Euro and Copa América data. A sequential FeatureBuilder enforces a strict read-then-update invariant, so features for match i use only matches 0 to i−1. 42 features cover Elo (with confederation-tuned K-factors), career goal statistics, recent form, tournament experience and head-to-head record, feeding a LightGBM classifier blended 75/25 with a Poisson expected-goals GLM, tuned over 300 Optuna trials.

Outcome. The model beats the majority-class baseline in all nine LOTO folds (1990 to 2022), averaging 52.4% accuracy with a +7.4pp lift. Interpretation surfaced a genuine signal: World Cup experience features outrank raw Elo, while confederation membership carries zero importance once performance features are included. The 100k-simulation bracket makes Brazil the most likely 2026 champion at 15.3%.
