ALICS: productionising computer vision for structural inspection

A cross-platform desktop application that takes research-grade crack detection models out of the lab and into the hands of field surveyors.

Problem. Three research computer-vision models (YOLO detection, U-Net segmentation, photometric-stereo 3D reconstruction) existed only as lab code. Civil engineers needed to run calibrated crack analysis offline, on site, and export engineering-grade reports, without installing Python or touching a terminal.

Approach. On a 16-week placement in a team of four, I built the entire application layer around the pre-existing analysis code: a Tauri 2 / Rust backend, a React 19 + TypeScript frontend, and a zero-copy Rust to Python inference bridge using PyO3 with NumPy ndarray views, so multi-megapixel images cross the language boundary without serialisation. A 12-migration SQLite layer persists per-scan, per-cell analysis results, including a BLOB migration that resolved row-size limits on large scans.

Outcome. Shipped as signed installers for Windows, macOS and Linux with a fully bundled Python runtime, so non-technical surveyors install the whole ML stack like any normal app. The tool outputs calibrated crack-width measurements in millimetres, a Three.js 3D mesh viewer with a signed depth scale, and templated PDF inspection reports suitable for structural documentation.

School connectivity optimisation under budget uncertainty

A multi-period MILP and two-stage stochastic programme allocating a $125M national budget to connect 12,578 Honduran schools to the internet.

Problem. Roughly 93% of Honduran public schools have no internet connection. Connectivity requires a three-layer prerequisite chain (tower, then WISP equipment, then school devices) with an 18:1 cost gap between equipping an existing tower and building a new one, phased over five years against an uncertain budget.

Approach. Our team formulated a multi-period mixed-integer linear programme in Julia/JuMP with Gurobi: budgeted maximal covering, prerequisite sequencing constraints, soft annual deployment milestones, and a two-stage stochastic extension hedging a ±20% budget shock in years 3 to 5. I built the geospatial data pipeline over 15,000+ schools and 7,297 candidate tower sites using a 10 km Haversine coverage radius.

Outcome. We solved the model twice on purpose: once unrestricted, connecting 98.7% of in-scope schools, and once with political deployment milestones, which costs 24.3 percentage points of final coverage, roughly 125 schools per point of early visibility. Comparing the two quantifies what year-on-year political progress actually costs. Both solutions are embedded in the dashboard below; switching scenario shows the 3,046-school gap materialise on the map. The stochastic extension adds a 1,067-school robust core immune to budget cuts, with EVPI and VSS bounds of at least 263 and 177 schools.

3 / 3Personal project · 2026

World Cup 2026 bracket predictor

A LightGBM + Poisson ensemble trained on 96 years of tournament history, validated with leave-one-tournament-out cross-validation and 100,000 Monte Carlo simulations.

Problem. Sports prediction is notorious for leakage and overfitting: features quietly built on future information, and test tournaments that bleed into training. The design constraint here was generalisation first, predictive power second.

Approach. Custom regex state machines parse 1,402 matches across every World Cup since 1930 plus Euro and Copa América data. A sequential FeatureBuilder enforces a strict read-then-update invariant, so features for match i use only matches 0 to i−1. 42 features cover Elo (with confederation-tuned K-factors), career goal statistics, recent form, tournament experience and head-to-head record, feeding a LightGBM classifier blended 75/25 with a Poisson expected-goals GLM, tuned over 300 Optuna trials.

Outcome. The model beats the majority-class baseline in all nine LOTO folds (1990 to 2022), averaging 52.4% accuracy with a +7.4pp lift. Interpretation surfaced a genuine signal: World Cup experience features outrank raw Elo, while confederation membership carries zero importance once performance features are included. The 100k-simulation bracket makes Brazil the most likely 2026 champion at 15.3%.
